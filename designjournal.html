The design journal was meant to be a place to "keep track of your ongoing experiments with digital tools, platforms, and assets encountered or inspired by the course readings and digital stories we explore together."

I'll be honest, for me I just treated it as a place to dump my assignments that were supposed to go into the design journal.

Design Journal #1 was supposed to be an exercise from Hagen & Golombisky's White Space is Not Your Enemy: A Beginner’s Guide to Communicating Visually through Graphic, Web, and Multimedia Design.. I chose an exercise on identifying Gestalt principles in corporate logos.

Design Journal #2 was to take a blog post we had made and expand on it to tie it into the history of electronic/digital literature.

Design Journal #3 gave me the option to just collect 2-3 of our favorite blog posts/reading responses from over the semester. Moving away from the medium of Notion gave me the ability to make my Hole in One poem a little more graphical – I'm quite happy with how it turned out.
